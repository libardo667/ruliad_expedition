The Actual Problem
Being constantly informed is not the same as understanding anything.

Decades of high-frequency media exposure have done something measurable to human cognition: they've trained our brains to mistake consumption for comprehension. The feeling of staying informed — the dopamine of the new, the anxiety of missing something — has replaced the slower, more uncertain process of actually sitting with complexity. The Ruliad Expedition Kit doesn't give you more information. It gives you a different relationship with the information you already have.

The Entrainment Problem

Mass media and the attention economy are extraordinarily efficient at synchronizing your cognitive patterns with their own. Outrage, novelty, tribal confirmation — your brain's threat-detection system gets recruited for engagement, not understanding. You feel informed. Your thinking has been narrowed.

The Ruliad Response

Instead of one perspective finding what it's looking for, eight independent disciplinary lenses fire simultaneously. Convergence becomes evidence. Contradiction becomes information. What no single perspective can see becomes visible only in the synthesis — the emergent structure of the thing itself.

The Single-Thread Problem

A single AI conversation is one perspective, one lens, one set of inherited assumptions. The model's default framing becomes yours. You get an answer that feels comprehensive. You get one slice of a much larger structure — and no way to see the rest.

FAQ
Common questions.
What API keys do I need?
The tool works with any OpenRouter-compatible API key, which gives you access to DeepSeek R1, Claude, GPT-4, Mistral, and dozens of other models. You can also use a direct Anthropic or OpenAI key. Embedding models and research models are configured separately — the guide walks you through it.
How much does each expedition cost in API fees?
Typically $0.20–$1.50 depending on the model and whether web search is enabled. DeepSeek R1 with web search (the default "rigor" mode) is on the lower end. Claude Opus with full grounding is on the higher end. You control the budget per run.
Do I need technical skills to use this?
You need to be comfortable running a command in a terminal (one command to start the proxy). The Founding Member tier includes a full video walkthrough. If you can install Node.js and paste an API key, you can run expeditions.
What can I actually scan?
Anything you'd research. Geopolitical disputes, regulatory questions, philosophical concepts, competitive landscapes, organizational risks, scientific controversies, policy proposals. The tool adapts its disciplinary probes to whatever you ask. Previous expeditions have scanned topics from "the meaning of life" to Pentagon–Anthropic AI policy disputes.
Is my data private?
The tool runs locally on your machine — there is no intermediary server between you and your chosen API provider, and your expedition data (the JSON, the visualization) stays on your local filesystem. However, your prompts are sent to whichever AI provider you configure. If you route through OpenRouter, your queries transit their infrastructure before reaching the model provider. Each provider has its own data handling policies and jurisdictional realities — DeepSeek is subject to Chinese law, Google retains API inputs for abuse monitoring, and so on. For maximum privacy, use a provider that offers zero-data-retention terms and connect via direct API rather than a routing service. The setup guide covers the privacy tradeoffs of each provider option.
What's the theoretical basis?
The methodology is called Ruliad Tomography — named after Stephen Wolfram's concept of the ruliad (the entangled limit of all possible computations). The core insight: LLMs trained on the full corpus of human symbolic output are holographic encodings of collective cognitive structure. By firing multiple independent probes and looking for convergence, you're performing computational tomography on a region of that space. Convergent findings across independent disciplinary lenses are evidence of genuine structural features — not artifacts of any single perspective.
Can I modify the tool?
It's a single HTML file and a proxy script. Everything is readable, modifiable, and yours. Add probes, change the visualization, pipe the JSON into your own analysis pipeline. No obfuscation, no DRM.
